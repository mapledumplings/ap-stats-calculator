Sample AP Statistics Inference Problems
=======================================

1) One-proportion z-test
In a random sample of 150 students, 102 say they get at least 8 hours of sleep
on school nights. A school claims that 60% of its students get at least 8 hours
of sleep. Test the claim at alpha = 0.05.

Suggested inputs:
- context: students at the school who get at least 8 hours of sleep
- x = 102
- n = 150
- p0 = 0.60
- tail = !=
- alpha = 0.05


2) One-proportion z-interval
A random sample of 250 voters shows that 140 support a new city policy.
Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of voters who
support the policy.

Suggested inputs:
- context: city voters who support the new policy
- x = 140
- n = 250
- confidence level = 0.95


3) One-mean t-test
A company says the mean wait time at a service desk is 8 minutes. A random
sample of 20 customers had a mean wait time of 9.4 minutes with sample
standard deviation 2.5 minutes. Test whether the true mean wait time is
greater than 8 minutes at alpha = 0.05.

Suggested inputs:
- context: customer wait time at the service desk
- x-bar = 9.4
- s = 2.5
- n = 20
- mu0 = 8
- tail = >
- alpha = 0.05


4) One-mean t-interval
A random sample of 16 batteries had a mean life of 41.2 hours with sample
standard deviation 3.6 hours. Construct a 90% confidence interval for the
true mean battery life.

Suggested inputs:
- context: battery life
- x-bar = 41.2
- s = 3.6
- n = 16
- confidence level = 0.90
